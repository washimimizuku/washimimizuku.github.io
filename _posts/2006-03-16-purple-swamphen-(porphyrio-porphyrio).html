--- layout: post title: Purple Swamphen (Porphyrio porphyrio) ---

The Purple Swamphen is a beautifull bird that can be found in many wet lands in Portugal. It's also a nice example of species recovery. Some years ago there were only a few of them in Portugal, and only one known breeding place, in Ludo, Ria Formosa. But now, this bird has extended his territory a lot. For example, it's really easy to see them in Lagoa de Albufeira, 300 km northwest of the Ria Formosa.

The best place to see them is Quinta do Lago, in the west end of Ria Formosa. There is a artificial lake there created for a golf course, where the Purple Swamphen (and many other interesting birds) can be seen easily. It's an interesting thought: tourism not only getting along with nature, but actually helping it creating habitats. An example to be followed.
