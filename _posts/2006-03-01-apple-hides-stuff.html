--- layout: post title: Apple hides stuff --- One thing I don't like about Apple, is that Apple hides stuff. No, I'm not talking about hiding what products are going to be released. What I'm talking about is when the hardware permits a certain functionality, and Apple takes that off using software.

I have a iBook G4, one of the firsts. The graphic card has dual screen capabilities, and a wide range of resolutions. But Apple decided that an iBook couldn't have it, so it turns it off in firmware. Luckily we have Screen Spanning Doctor to the rescue, which turns it on as it should be.

Yesterday they launched the new Mac mini with the Intel processor. In the middle of all the features, now it has a onboard graphics processor that uses the system RAM. OK, a marketing decision, I can live with that. But why would they force the ram used to be 64Mb when the onboard Intel GMA950 could use a maximum of 224Mb? OK, I agree this is not a very important thing. Not like the iBook one is. But the principle is the same.
