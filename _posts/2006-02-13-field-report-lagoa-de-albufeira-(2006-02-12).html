--- layout: post title: 'Field Report: Lagoa de Albufeira (2006-02-12)' --- On saturday morning I went to Lagoa de Albufeira. It's an interesting spot for bird wacthing. We can see great numbers og Grey Herons, Common Coots and Great Cormorants. This time I saw the bigger flock of Great Cormorants I have ever seen, probably in migration. And it's the only place in Portugal (not counting Algarve) where you can see the Purple Swamphen regularly. It is also a great place to see the Sandwich Tern fishing.

List of species seen:

Chaffinch (Fringilla coelebs) - 1
Sanderling (Calidris alba) - 10
Kentish Plover (Charadrius alexandrinus) - 10
Common Raven (Corvus corax) - 4
Caspian Gull (Larus cachinnans) - 30
Great Cormorant (Phalacrocorax carbo) - ~60
Black-headed Gull (Larus ridibundus) - 3
Robin (Erithacus rubecula) - 1
Eurasian Blackbird (Turdus merula) - 3
Grey Heron (Ardea cinerea) - 10
White Wagtail (Motacilla alba) - 2
Northern Shoveler (Anas clypeata) - 2
Gadwall (Anas strepera) - 2
Common Coot (Fulica atra) - ~200
Black-necked Grebe (Podiceps nigricollis) - 3
Purple Swamphen (Porphyrio porphyrio) - 1
Common Chiffchaff (Phylloscopus collybita) - 1
Sandwich Tern (Sterna sandvicensis) - 3
